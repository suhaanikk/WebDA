Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our Solar System and the closest to the Sun.
It has no atmosphere to retain heat, making it a planet of extremes.
During the day, the surface temperature can reach up to 430°C (800°F), but at night, it drops to -180°C (-290°F).
Mercury has a rocky surface covered in craters, resembling Earth's Moon.

Interesting Facts:
A year on Mercury is just 88 Earth days.
Mercury has the largest temperature difference of any planet in the Solar System.
Despite being closest to the Sun, it is not the hottest planet—that title goes to Venus.
