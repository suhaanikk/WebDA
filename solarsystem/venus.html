Venus

Venus is often called Earth's twin because of its similar size and composition.
However, the planet's atmosphere is incredibly thick and made mostly of carbon dioxide, trapping heat through a runaway greenhouse effect.
Venus rotates very slowly, taking 243 Earth days to complete one rotation, and its day is longer than its year.

Interesting Facts:
- Venus rotates in the opposite direction to most planets (retrograde rotation).
- The surface temperature of Venus can reach 465°C (869°F), hot enough to melt lead.
- Venus has mountains, volcanoes, and craters, but no liquid water.
